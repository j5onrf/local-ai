what is snow?

### Agent:
Snow is a form of precipitation consisting of tiny ice crystals that form when water vapor in the atmosphere freezes onto dust particles or other nuclei, typically at temperatures below freezing. These crystals grow into the characteristic six-sided hexagonal shapes that give snowflakes their intricate patterns, then fall to the ground in clusters called snowflakes. Snow accumulates and forms snowpack when temperatures remain cold enough to prevent melting, and it's responsible for the white blanket covering landscapes during winter, the whiteout conditions of blizzards, and the crystalline beauty of frost patterns on windows.
