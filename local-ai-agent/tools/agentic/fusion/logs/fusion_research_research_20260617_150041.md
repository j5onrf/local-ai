````
**Baking Soda (NaHCO₃)**  
- **How it works:** Single‑acting alkaline; reacts instantly when it contacts an acid (e.g., lemon juice, yogurt, buttermilk, honey, cocoa). Produces CO₂ that leavens the batter.  
- **When to use:** Recipes that already contain enough acid; mix just before baking, otherwise the gas dissipates and the product can taste metallic.  

**Baking Powder**  
- **How it works:** Double‑acting mix of baking soda, a dry acid (cream of tartar or monocalcium phosphate) and a filler (starch). – *First rise* when wet, – *second rise* when heated.  
- **When to use:** Neutral‑flavored batters that lack added acid; provides leavening throughout mixing and baking.  

**Key difference:** Soda needs an external acid and reacts immediately; powder contains its own acid and activates in two stages, giving more flexibility and a slower, steadier rise.
````
